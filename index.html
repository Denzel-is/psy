1. Early and Medieval States

  • Saka tribes are mentioned in the Persian source Avesta.
  • The Turkic Khagans belonged to the Ashina clan.
  • The Karakhanid State existed on the territory of Kazakhstan in 942–1210.
  • The ulus that included northern Zhetysu and Eastern Desht-i-Kipchak was the Jochi Ulus.
  • The state of Abulkhair Khan existed in 1428–1467.

2. Russian Empire and Anti-Colonial Resistance

  • The reforms of the Russian Empire weakened traditional Kazakh power. The khan’s power in the Middle Zhuz was abolished in 1822; in the Junior Zhuz in 1824.
  • The uprising led by Syrym Datula had a long duration, local Junior Zhuz coverage, anti-colonial character and anti-feudal character.
  • Kenesary Kasymov is remembered as the “Rebellious Sultan”.
  • Leaders of the 1916 uprising include Amangeldy Imanov, Myrzhakyp Dulatov and Tokash Bokin.

3. Alash Movement and Kazakh Intelligentsia

  • Forms of struggle of Kazakh intelligentsia included petition campaigns, participation in the State Duma, and publication of periodicals.
  • The Alash Party supported the idea of territorial autonomy.
  • According to A. Bukeikhanov, serving the nation depends not so much on knowledge as on character.
  • Figures who were not Alash supporters include S. Seifullin, T. Ryskulov and A. Zhangeldin.

4. Soviet Modernization

  • War Communism included surplus appropriation and labor service. In your added cards, food tax is also included according to the provided answer key.
  • The New Economic Policy led to an increase in sown areas, livestock, and middle-peasant farms.
  • Soviet economic modernization in the 1920s–1930s included collectivization, industrialization and urbanization.
  • Collectivization created collective farms and was marked by violent methods, forced pace, and ignoring the population’s way of life.
  • Industrialization means creating machine-based production.

5. Soviet Political System and Khrushchev Period

  • Main features of the Soviet political system: cult of personality, political repression, and Communist Party monopoly.
  • The Khrushchev Thaw included attempts to modernize the Soviet economy, criticism of Stalin’s personality cult, and rehabilitation of victims of Stalinism.
  • Reasons for the ineffectiveness of Khrushchev’s reforms: voluntarism, low productivity, inconsistency.
  • In industrial development among USSR republics, Kazakhstan occupied the 3rd place.

6. Culture, Science and National Identity

  • After October 1917, cultural transformation focused on overcoming mass illiteracy and developing proletarian culture.
  • In 1946, the Academy of Sciences of the Kazakh SSR was established.
  • Rukhani Zhangyru is connected with modernization of public consciousness.
  • Key areas of national identity modernization: competitiveness, cult of knowledge, and pragmatism.

7. Independent Kazakhstan and Foreign Policy

  • Kazakhstan’s foreign policy is multi-vector, proactive, and pragmatic.
  • Kazakhstan is a member of the UN, OIC and OSCE.
  • Kazakhstan chaired the OSCE in 2010.
  • Relations with the European Union are based on the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement.
  • The December 1986 events contributed to national identity, democratic processes and Kazakhstan’s path toward independence.
